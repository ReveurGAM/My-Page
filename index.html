About Me

Hey! I'm Glenn. I started using computers in the late '70s, and I started learning about programming in 1980, although I only really got started in 1984. I enjoyed the challenges presented by coding, so I went to college and learned more, and I helped one of my teachers with documentation of his grading program. I also focused on writing and science, and even learned a bit about circuitry.
From around '90-95, I ran a BBS called the Tower of Babble on my Apple computers (a Franklin ACE 500, then a IIe and a IIgs), which ran on the modified ACOS (MACOS) language. With the help of the SysOp of Far East Network, Scott Klement, who is a very skilled programmer, I learned about ACOS and a bit about hardware. I helped other people set up their own BBSes, too, but Scott was the main programmer - he even rebuilt my BBS a couple of times when the files were corrupted. Eventually, I enlisted Brian Brechlin, who is a tech head/engineer now, to be my co-SysOp in charge of new user admissions. Although my website offered a variety of things, from forums and games to text and program file libraries, it eventually lost popularity and I ended up taking it down as a result.
I am pretty good with hardware, and somewhat about finding and eliminating malware, although I'm mostly self-taught. I learned about remote malware fighting on www.bleepingcomputer.com for a while, too. Unfortunately, I know very little about networking. I am currently trying to get a certification, probably MS 70:480 or maybe CompTIA A+, Network+ or something else.
I have spent several weeks in Singapore, and I lived in Indonesia for 15.5 years, which was a very interesting experience. I learned about other languages, cultures, religions and history, as well as food and art! I have two children who are half-Indonesian; their mother died in March 2017 of hypoglycemic shock (although the presiding doctor falsely reported that it was her stage 4 cervical cancer that killed her).
